3. competitor landscaping and analysis

There seemed to be two main tradeoffs between existing solutions: solutions that could help students physically get home safely, such as shuttles, campus walking partners, or even Ubers. These solutions were costly and could take up a lot of time in later hours. Other solutions that were crime notifier either had confusing user interfaces or reported crime after the fact. Within crime reporting notification services, the reports seemed to have two main tradeoffs: accurate and verified reports vs. real-time notifications.

Examining the competitive landscape revealed opportunities that our app could address.

5. lessons learned

A product is not complete when you have nothing less to add- a product is complete when you have nothing left to take away. Don't create a swiss army knife (unless that's your product purpose.) Choose one or two functions that provide the most utility to users and deliver on those features.
